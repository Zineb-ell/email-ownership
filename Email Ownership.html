The Problem — No Inbox Ownership
Starting point
▼

The CEO had two active business inboxes. He was reading them sporadically — no structure, no filtering, no dedicated owner.

Risk 1Real opportunities being missed — partnerships, legal notices, strategic deals
Risk 2CEO constantly distracted by noise — receipts, system alerts, cold outreach
Risk 3No one owned the inbox — emails fell through the cracks
Risk 4Two inboxes — two business units — no unified process
1
CEO Brief — Analyze First, Then Propose
CEO instruction
▼

The CEO gave a clear 2-phase instruction: first review without touching anything, then come back with a concrete proposal through the Daily Brief.

RuleDo not change anything during analysis — review only
Phase 1Analyse both inboxes — Tue & Wed
Phase 2Submit proposal via Daily Brief — by Thursday morning
End GoalFull inbox ownership transferred to EA after alignment

Phase 1 — Inbox Analysis (Review Only)
Tue + Wed
▼

Full review of both inboxes — no changes made. Every email type categorized into 4 groups.

🔴 CEO Must SeePartnerships, legal docs, finance alerts, strategic opportunities, HR decisions, Airbnb account flags
🟢 RoutableFinance receipts, booking confirmations, ops updates, system alerts → route to correct team
⚙️ Auto-FilterVerification codes, Slack notifications, login alerts, building notices → skip inbox automatically
🗑️ UnsubscribeCold outreach, newsletters, irrelevant promotions → permanently removed
